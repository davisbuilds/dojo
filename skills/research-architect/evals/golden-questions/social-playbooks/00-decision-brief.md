# Stage 0 — Decision Brief

**Topic:** Social media marketing tactics, cross-platform strategies, and brand building — what practitioners like MrBeast (Jimmy Donaldson), Alex Hormozi, and Lulu Cheng Meservey actually do versus what they say, and how the effective playbook has changed over time.

**Decision informed:** Findings get encoded into the `viral` skill kit — its content skills, rules (e.g. anti-slop, voice-discipline), and platform playbooks. The report's build handoff should map validated tactics to specific skills/rules to create or update. Actors: the repo maintainer (Davis) and the agents that run the kit. Timeframe: near-term; the kit is under active development.

**Cost of being wrong:** Encoding survivorship theater into reusable skills poisons every future draft the kit produces, at scale. Staleness: tactic-level evidence should be from the last 12–18 months; fundamentals may be older if durability is itself evidenced.

**Useful null result:** "Expert tactics are mostly non-transferable or decayed; the durable core is small and boring." That would refocus the kit on fundamentals and platform-native mechanics instead of guru tactics — a success, not a failure.

**Priors (targets for disconfirmation, not confirmation):**

1. Founder-led/"going direct" beats brand accounts (the Meservey thesis).
2. Retention mechanics — hook, pacing, payoff — are the core transferable craft (the MrBeast thesis); platform specifics are secondary.
3. Volume + repurposing wins: one pillar piece atomized across platforms, quantity compounds (the Hormozi thesis).
4. Most public advice is survivorship theater: what experts say differs from what they did, and tactics decay within months of publication.

**What would genuinely surprise:**

(a) the experts' outcomes are mostly explained by capital, timing, or pre-existing position — not replicable craft;
(b) the "how it changed over time" story is mostly noise and the same 3–4 principles from ~2015 still explain outcomes.

**Execution surface (from stage-0 routing input):** both a local terminal agent run AND one web DR product — so stage 4 produces two calibrated prompts.
