# Stage 1 — Question Engineering

## Core question

Which specific tactics used by MrBeast, Alex Hormozi, and Lulu Cheng Meservey are
independently corroborated as causally responsible for their results and are
transferable to a solo/small-team creator via encodable rules — versus survivorship
theater, capital/timing-dependent, or decayed since publication — and how has the
transferable core shifted since ~2015?

## Sub-questions, in priority order

1. For each practitioner, what specific, checkable tactics did they actually execute
   (from primary sources: production docs, verified interviews, replicated case
   studies) — as distinct from what they say in guru/course content?
2. Which of those tactics are independently corroborated (multiple non-affiliated
   sources, ideally outcome data) versus resting only on the practitioner's own claim
   or a channel with a financial interest in the claim?
3. Which tactics are platform-specific mechanics (algorithm/format exploits) versus
   general craft principles (retention, hook design, distribution volume/cadence) —
   and does that split predict transferability across the kit's seven platforms?
4. How has each practitioner's stated *and* actual playbook changed 2015→2026 — do
   "timeless principle" claims hold up, or is what demonstrably worked in 2018
   verifiably different from what works now?
5. For each of the four priors (going-direct beats brand accounts; retention mechanics
   are the transferable core; volume/repurposing compounds; most public advice is
   survivorship theater), what's the strongest disconfirming evidence, and does the
   prior hold across all seven kit platforms or only the practitioner's home platform?
6. What confounds — capital, timing, pre-existing audience/position, one-time
   algorithm/platform windows — explain each practitioner's headline result better
   than the claimed tactic?
7. Which validated (non-theater, non-confound-explained) tactics map cleanly onto a
   specific, buildable rule or skill behavior for the `viral` kit, and which are too
   context-dependent (require ongoing human judgment, not a rule) to encode?

## Scope

**In scope:**
- Organic content tactics and strategy on the kit's seven platforms: X, YouTube,
  Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook, Substack, Reddit.
- Founder-led / "going direct" posture vs. brand-account posture.
- Hook, pacing, retention, and format craft.
- Repurposing/atomization pipelines (one piece → many platform-native derivatives).
- Comms strategy (Meservey) insofar as it shapes organic social/content behavior
  (press posture, "going direct" as distribution, narrative control) — not general PR
  crisis management.
- Publishing cadence, volume, and production process (including the MrBeast
  production-guide material, subject to source verification per do-nots below).
- Voice/authenticity signals and their measurable effect on engagement or trust.
- Algorithm-adjacent mechanics only as they affect organic reach (not paid delivery).

**Out of scope:**
- Paid ads/boosted spend and ad-platform mechanics, except where organic-style
  content is seeded with paid amplification and that fact changes how the organic
  tactic should be read (flag, don't deep-dive).
- General SEO outside social platforms.
- Email/CRM/funnel marketing, except where it is structurally a Substack mechanic.
- Hormozi's business acquisition/PE/portfolio-company operating tactics unrelated to
  content or distribution.
- Legal/PR crisis-management specifics beyond what's germane to content/distribution
  strategy.
- Monetization mechanics (creator funds, brand-deal economics) except where they
  structurally change content incentives (e.g., a format exists because it pays, not
  because it retains).

## Classification scheme

Unlike things are being compared here — a MrBeast video, a Hormozi repurposing
pipeline, and a Meservey comms strategy are different *kinds* of phenomena. Tag every
significant example on these four axes:

1. **Tactic type** — `content-craft` (hook/pacing/format decisions) | `production-system`
   (pipeline/process/org) | `distribution-posture` (founder-led vs. brand, platform
   selection, cadence) | `comms-positioning` (narrative control, press/going-direct
   posture).
2. **Platform-generality** — `platform-specific mechanic` | `cross-platform craft
   principle` | `meta-strategic posture` (operates above the platform layer, e.g. brand
   architecture).
3. **Evidence type** — `practitioner's own claim/content` | `leaked or reconstructed
   internal process` | `independently observed/replicated outcome` | `academic or
   analytics-firm data`.
4. **Transferability** — `directly encodable as a kit rule/skill` | `requires resources
   the kit's users don't have` (budget, team, pre-existing platform position) |
   `context-dependent` (needs ongoing human judgment, not a rule).

**Category-error example this scheme is meant to expose:** filing a $2M MrBeast
thumbnail A/B-testing system and a solo creator's hook-writing habit under the same
"retention craft" bucket without the transferability axis erases the fact that one
requires infrastructure the kit's users don't have and the other doesn't. Likewise,
conflating Meservey's PR-crisis media strategy (comms-positioning, low platform-
generality) with everyday founder posting cadence (distribution-posture, high
platform-generality) would produce a false equivalence the axes are designed to catch.

## Rubric (acceptance criteria — checkable from report text alone)

1. Every tactic attributed to MrBeast, Hormozi, or Meservey cites a primary or
   independently-corroborated source, dated, and explicitly distinguishes "said" from
   "did."
2. Each of the four priors (going-direct; retention-mechanics-as-core; volume/
   repurposing compounds; survivorship-theater) has its own subsection presenting both
   supporting and disconfirming evidence — not supporting evidence alone.
3. At least one documented platform-transfer failure (a tactic that worked on the
   practitioner's home platform and demonstrably underperformed or failed elsewhere)
   appears per practitioner.
4. Every significant example is tagged on all four classification axes (tactic type,
   platform-generality, evidence type, transferability).
5. The report distinguishes tactics that changed materially in the last 12–18 months
   from those stable since ~2015–2018, with dated evidence for any change claim.
6. A negative-evidence section (churn, backlash, format fatigue, algorithm penalty,
   public walk-backs) appears for each of the three practitioners.
7. The build handoff maps each validated (non-theater) tactic to a specific named
   `viral`-kit skill or rule (`hook-engine`, `platform-playbook`, `post-scorer`,
   `post-writer`, `repurpose`, `voice-context`, `rules/anti-slop`,
   `rules/output-location`, `rules/social-conventions`, `rules/voice-discipline`) or
   explicitly proposes a new one — never a generic "consider doing X" recommendation.
8. For each practitioner's headline result, the report explicitly assesses whether
   capital, timing, or pre-existing position explains the result better than the
   claimed tactic.
9. The report states an explicit verdict on the null-result question — whether the
   durable transferable core is "small and boring" or substantial — not just
   descriptive findings without a conclusion.

## Topic do-nots

- Do not treat MrBeast's leaked/circulated "creator playbook" documents as verified
  without checking their republication chain — much of this circulates as unverified
  screenshots or PDFs of uncertain provenance.
- Do not treat Hormozi's own courses, books, or channel content about his own methods
  as independent evidence of efficacy — he sells attention/content instruction, which
  is a direct financial interest in the claim (recursive-grift risk).
- Do not treat Lulu Cheng Meservey's Substack (*Rough Drafts*) or interviews as neutral
  commentary — she runs a comms firm (Rostra) that sells "going direct" as a service;
  her advocacy carries a financial interest in the thesis being validated.
- Do not conflate MrBeast's production-budget- and team-size-dependent tactics with
  tactics a solo or small-team creator can actually replicate.
- Do not present platform algorithm mechanics (which change frequently and are largely
  NDA'd/undisclosed) as more certain than they are — label every mechanic as inferred
  or confirmed, and date it.
- Do not treat a single viral hit as evidence of a repeatable system — require repeated
  documented success before calling a tactic validated.
